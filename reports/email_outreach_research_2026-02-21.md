# Email Outreach Research Notes (2026-02-21)

Scope: practical, specialist-backed rules for demo-first cold outreach to personal trainers.

## Rules used in generator v2

1. Keep one low-friction CTA in first email (permission to send mini-audit, not hard close).
   - Source: HubSpot follow-up guide
   - https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/how-to-send-a-follow-up-email-after-no-response

2. Keep subject line short and specific; avoid clickbait.
   - Source: Mailshake lesson + HubSpot subject line guidance
   - https://mailshake.com/masterclass/write-cold-email/
   - https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/sales-email-subject-lines-that-get-prospects-to-open-read-and-respond

3. Personalization must be contextual (offer cue, niche cue, concrete observation), not only name/company.
   - Source: Mailshake personalization lesson
   - https://mailshake.com/masterclass/email-personalization/

4. First line should reflect recipient problem/context, not sender biography.
   - Source: Mailshake writing framework
   - https://mailshake.com/masterclass/write-cold-email/

5. Follow-up sequence is mandatory; one email is rarely enough.
   - Source: Yesware follow-up analysis
   - https://www.yesware.com/blog/follow-up-email/

6. Every follow-up must add new value (new quick win, new angle), not "just checking in".
   - Source: Yesware follow-up mistakes
   - https://www.yesware.com/blog/follow-up-email/

7. Use timing discipline and iterate from data.
   - Source: Yesware timing and reporting guidance
   - https://www.yesware.com/blog/best-time-to-send-email-data/

8. Keep curiosity and relevance; do not over-explain in first touch.
   - Source: Lavender writing guidance
   - https://www.lavender.ai/blog/cold-email-best-practices

9. Build niche-specific messaging to avoid generic templates.
   - Source: REPLY method (Jason Bay)
   - https://gtmnow.com/how-to-create-your-own-cold-email-template-using-the-reply-method/

## Practical constraints used in this project

- Demo-first flow: D0 + D+2 + D+5 + D+9
- One CTA per message
- Per-lead quick win in first and second follow-up
- Confidence flag (high/medium/low) based on data quality (website vs social-only)
